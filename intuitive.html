üß† Daily System Check: Your Internal Coach

Embrace the "train by feel" approach. Every morning, answer these three questions before deciding on your workout intensity.

1. The Physical Scan

Where is the pain/soreness? If it's **sharp pain**, the answer is **rest**. If it's *fatigue/soreness*, a light run might be beneficial.

2. The Mental/Stressor Scan

Am I burnt out, stressed, or dreading the run? If so, **cut the run in half** or change it to a fun, low-impact activity.

3. The Hunger/Energy Scan

Am I well-fueled and rested? If running on fumes, a hard effort will do more harm. Choose an **Easy Run or Rest** instead.

üí™ Daily Activation Routine (15-20 Min)

Perform this routine **7 days a week** before your run or later in the day. Focus on **control and form** (2-3 Sets of 15-20 Reps).

1. Single-Leg Glute Bridges

Focus: Glute Max & Hamstrings

Lift hips with both feet, then extend one leg. Drive back up, squeezing the glute of the planted foot. Keep hips level and square.

2. Clamshells

Focus: Glute Medius (Hip Stabilizer)

Lie on your side with knees bent. Keep feet together and lift the top knee. Use a band for more resistance. Do not rock your torso.

3. Bird-Dogs

Focus: Core & Posterior Chain

Start on all fours. Extend opposite arm/leg parallel to the floor. Avoid arching your lower back. Hold for a 2-second pause at extension.

4. Side Planks (w/ Rotation)

Focus: Obliques & Hip Abductors

Hold a straight side plank line. For rotation, thread your top arm under your body, then raise it back up. Maintain a rigid core.

5. Single-Leg Deadlifts (SLDL)

Focus: Balance & Hamstring/Glute Strength

Hinge at the hip, keeping a flat back, extending one leg behind you. Go slowly and focus on balancing on the planted foot.

6. Single-Leg Calf Raises

Focus: Calf & Ankle Stability

Stand on one foot. Slowly raise yourself onto the ball of your foot (tip-toes), hold briefly, and lower slowly with control. Important for trail stability.
