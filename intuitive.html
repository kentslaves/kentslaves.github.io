üß† Daily System Check: Your Internal Coach

Embrace the "train by feel" approach. Every morning, answer these three questions before deciding on your workout intensity.

1. The Physical Scan

Where is the pain/soreness? If it's **sharp pain**, the answer is **rest**. If it's *fatigue/soreness*, a light run might be beneficial.

2. The Mental/Stressor Scan

Am I burnt out, stressed, or dreading the run? If so, **cut the run in half** or change it to a fun, low-impact activity.

3. The Hunger/Energy Scan

Am I well-fueled and rested? If running on fumes, a hard effort will do more harm. Choose an **Easy Run or Rest** instead.

üí™ Strength Protocol: 2x Focus & Daily Activation

We're adopting a high-consistency, multi-pronged strength approach to maximize durability and power.

1. Dedicated Strength (2x / Week)

These are focused, 30-45 minute sessions done on non-priority days (like Easy Run days or rest days). Use light weights or body weight, focusing on **single-leg stability, glute strength, and core anti-rotation**.

  • **Focus 1:** Glutes, Hips, Core Endurance (e.g., Clamshells, Weighted Glute Bridges, Plank Variations).
  • **Focus 2:** Single-Leg Stability & Vertical Strength (e.g., Bulgarian Split Squats, Single-Leg Deadlifts, Step-Ups).

2. Pre-Run Activation (Before Every Run)

This is your 5-minute Dauwalter-style movement. The goal is to "wake up" the muscles (especially the glutes and core) *before* you start running, ensuring they fire correctly and take the load off your knees/ankles. **Do this before your warmup.**

  • **5-Minute Routine:** 15 Glute Bridges, 15 Bird Dogs (per side), 10 Clamshells (per side), 10 Walking Lunges.
  • **Mental Cue:** Turn on the engine before driving the car.
