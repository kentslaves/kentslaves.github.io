üß† Daily System Check: Your Internal Coach

Embrace the "train by feel" approach. Every morning, answer these three questions before deciding on your workout intensity.

1. The Physical Scan

Where is the pain/soreness? If it's **sharp pain**, the answer is **rest**. If it's *fatigue/soreness*, a light run might be beneficial.

2. The Mental/Stressor Scan

Am I burnt out, stressed, or dreading the run? If so, **cut the run in half** or change it to a fun, low-impact activity.

3. The Hunger/Energy Scan

Am I well-fueled and rested? If running on fumes, a hard effort will do more harm. Choose an **Easy Run or Rest** instead.

üí™ Strength Protocol: Lower/Upper Split & Activation

We adopt a split strength approach to maximize durability. **Dedicated Strength** sessions should be 30-45 minutes and done on non-priority days.

1. Lower Body Focus (Legs & Glutes)

Focus on building **single-leg stability, glute power, and calf resilience**. This is your main injury-proofing session.

  • **Key Moves:** Bulgarian Split Squats, Single-Leg Deadlifts, Step-Ups, Calf Raises, Clamshells.

2. Upper Body Focus (Core & Back)

Focus on **core anti-rotation, back endurance, and posture**. Critical for holding form and carrying a pack late in the race.

  • **Key Moves:** Plank Variations, Bird Dog, Rows (e.g., Dumbbell or Band), Overhead Press (light weight).

3. Pre-Run Activation (Before Every Run)

This is your 5-minute movement. The goal is to "wake up" the muscles (especially the glutes and core) *before* you start running. **Do this before your warmup.**

  • **5-Minute Routine:** 15 Glute Bridges, 15 Bird Dogs (per side), 10 Clamshells (per side), 10 Walking Lunges.
