üß† Daily System Check: Your Internal Coach

Embrace the "train by feel" approach. Every morning, answer these three questions before deciding on your workout intensity.

1. The Physical Scan

Where is the pain/soreness? If it's **sharp pain**, the answer is **rest**. If it's *fatigue/soreness*, a light run might be beneficial.

2. The Mental/Stressor Scan

Am I burnt out, stressed, or dreading the run? If so, **cut the run in half** or change it to a fun, low-impact activity.

3. The Hunger/Energy Scan

Am I well-fueled and rested? If running on fumes, a hard effort will do more harm. Choose an **Easy Run or Rest** instead.

üí™ 30-Minute Ultra-Strength Session (Dauwalter Style)

Courtney's approach prioritizes **efficiency** and **longevity** over heavy lifting. This routine focuses on the core, hips, and single-leg stability‚Äîthe key components for downhill running resilience and injury prevention.

When to Schedule This (1-2x/Week)

  • **Recommendation:** Pair with a **Fun Run** (Tuesday or Friday) after the run is complete.
  • **Alternative:** Schedule it on a **Rest Day** to ensure maximal separation from your hard running efforts.
  • **Duration:** 20-30 minutes, 1-2 times per week.

üåü Lifestyle & Recovery Pillars

Fueling (The Fun Way)

  • Eat what feels right for daily life‚Äîdon't restrict.
  • Practice eating the "fun foods" (candy, simple carbs) on long runs.
  • Hydrate constantly, not just during runs.

Recovery & Mindset

  • **Sleep is #1:** Take extra sleep over a scheduled run if needed.
  • Keep gear simple and comfortable; reduce decision fatigue.
  • Embrace the discomfort: Use the Dauwalter mantra ("I'm fine," "Everything is fine") when the Pain Cave comes calling.
